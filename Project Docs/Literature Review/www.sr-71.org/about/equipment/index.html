As of February 2006, I am shooting with two digital SLR bodies, the Nikon D100 and D200. However, much of what you will find on this web site is not from these bodies, but from an older point and shoot digital camera, the Canon PowerShot S100 (also used to take the pictures below).

My first DSLR, I acquired the Nikon D100 in April 2003. I used it extensively until acquiring the Nikon D200 in December 2005. I will continue to use the D100 for midrange zooms with the 24-85mm lens that I keep attached to it at all times. However, much of the work I do is with my ultra-wide-angle 12-24mm DX Nikkor. This lens allows me to work in tight situations, such as in museums where large aircraft are closely adjacent to one another. I will be keeping this on the D200 as much as possible.

In addition, I also carry a telephoto lens, the 70-200mm VR Nikkor. This fast (f/2.8) lens is great for just about any situation. I only wish I could get out more with it.

Also, I carry the Nikon SB-800 Speedlight for daylight fill flash, as well as a number of other accessories like the MC-30 remote release for the D200 as well as a B+W circular polarizing filter, Hoya UV filter, Canon 500D CU filter, and the Expodisc automatic white balance tool.
